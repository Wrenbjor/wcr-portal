# Cold Call Script — South Jersey Trades Outreach

> **Framework:** Hormozi CLOSER (Clarify → Label → Overview → Sell the Vacation → Explain Away → Reinforce)
> **Tonality:** Concerned curiosity when asking about their business. Confident authority when presenting the solution. Calm certainty when handling objections. Never adversarial — you're a doctor prescribing, not a salesman pushing.
> **Rule:** Spend 70% of the call on THEIR situation, 30% on your solution.
> **Placeholders:** `{{Rep Name}}` = the sales rep or AI agent making the call. `{{First Name}}`, `{{Business Name}}`, `{{Trade Type}}`, `{{City}}` = lead data.

---

## Before You Call

- Have their preview site open on your screen
- Know their name, business name, trade, city, and Google review count
- Call Tuesday–Thursday, 7:00–8:00 AM or 4:30–6:00 PM
- Stand up when you call — it changes your voice
- Have their competitor's Google results pulled up (you'll reference this)

---

## C — Clarify Why They Should Care (First 15 Seconds)

> "Hey {{First Name}}, this is {{Rep Name}} from WCR Studios — we're a web design company here in South Jersey that works with local {{Trade Type}} businesses. The reason I'm calling: we actually built a website for {{Business Name}} and I wanted to get your honest take on it. Got about two minutes?"

**If "Who is this? / What's this about?"** (Concerned curiosity tone)

> "Totally fair — I'm {{Rep Name}} with WCR Studios. We build websites for trades businesses in South Jersey. We put together a site for {{Business Name}} to show you what we can do. I'm not trying to sell you anything on this call — I genuinely just want your opinion on it."

**If "I'm busy"**

> "Completely understand — you're working. When's a better time for two minutes? I've got something ready for you that I think you'll want to see."

*(Book a specific callback time. Don't say "I'll try again later.")*

---

## L — Label Their Problem (30 Seconds)

> "So here's what made me reach out: I searched '{{Trade Type}} near me' in {{City}} this morning. Three businesses showed up — and {{Business Name}} wasn't one of them. That means right now, when someone in your area needs a {{Trade Type}} and pulls out their phone, they're calling whoever Google puts in front of them first. Does that sound about right?"

*(Pause. Let them respond. If they say "yeah" or "I guess so" — you're winning. If they push back, go to Overview.)*

> "And here's the thing that costs you — it's not just new customers. Even your referrals are Googling you before they call. If nothing comes up, or they see a competitor with a nicer site, that referral walks. You'll never even know it happened."

---

## O — Overview Their Current Situation

> "What are you doing right now to get new customers? Mostly word of mouth, or are you doing anything online?"

*(Shut up and listen. They'll tell you everything you need. Mirror their words back.)*

**If they say "Word of mouth":**

> "That's how most trades guys do it — and it works. The issue is it's unpredictable. Some months are great, some months are slow, and you can't control which. A website doesn't replace referrals — it makes sure every referral actually converts and brings in the people who don't know your name yet."

**If they say "I have a website":**

> "Awesome — you're ahead of most. When's the last time you looked at it on your phone? A lot of trades sites were built 3-4 years ago and they're not really set up for how people search now. If yours is solid, I'll tell you that straight up."

**If they say "I tried a website before, didn't do anything":**

> "That makes sense — and that's actually the #1 thing I hear. Most trades websites are basically digital business cards. They look okay but they're not built to actually rank on Google or convert a visitor into a phone call. That's the difference with what I do."

---

## S — Sell the Vacation (Not the Plane Ride)

> "Picture this: it's a Tuesday morning, you're on a job, and your phone rings. It's someone in {{City}} who just Googled '{{Trade Type}} near me,' found your site, and hit the call button. You didn't run an ad. You didn't ask for a referral. They just found you. That's what a site built the right way does — it works for you 24/7, even when you're up on a roof or under a sink."

> "The site I built for you is already set up for exactly that. Want me to text you the link right now so you can see it?"

*(Send the link immediately if they say yes. Keep talking while they look at it.)*

> "You'll see your services, your service area with a Google Map, and a click-to-call button that works on every phone. The whole thing loads in under 2 seconds."

---

## E — Explain Away Concerns

> "What questions do you have for me about the site?"

*(NOT "what do you think?" — that invites wishy-washy answers. "What questions?" assumes forward motion.)*

### Objection 1: PRICE — "How much does it cost?" / "That's expensive."

> "The starter package is $499 for the full site and design, plus $50 a month for hosting, security, and basic maintenance. No contracts, no hidden fees."

**If "That's a lot":**

> "I hear you. Let me ask you this — what's the average job worth for you? $300? $500? So if this site brings you even one extra call this month, it's paid for itself. And most of my clients tell me they start getting calls within the first few weeks. This isn't an expense — it's the cheapest employee you'll ever hire, and it works 24/7."

> "I also never discount — but what I can do is include Google Business Profile setup for free if you move forward this week. That alone is worth $200 if you hired someone to do it."

### Objection 2: PARTNER/SPOUSE — "I need to talk to my wife/business partner."

> "Totally understand. When you talk to them, what do you think they'll say?"

*(If positive:)* "Would it help if I sent them the preview link too so they can see exactly what we're talking about?"

*(If uncertain:)* "What specifically would they be concerned about — the cost, or whether it'll actually work?" *(Then handle whichever one it is.)*

> "Most of the time, the partner just wants to know it's not a scam and it's a real investment. The preview site does that work for you — send it to them."

### Objection 3: TIME — "I'll think about it." / "Not the right time."

> "Respect that. What specifically do you want to think about?"

*(Force specificity. Vague objections get vague answers.)*

> "Is it the site itself, the price, or whether it'll actually bring in calls?"

*(Handle whichever one they name. Then:)*

> "Here's the thing — the best time to get a site up is before you need it. Once the slow season hits and you're wishing the phone was ringing, that's when everyone calls me. The guys who are booked solid right now? They got their sites up six months ago."

### Objection 4: TRUST — "How do I know this is legit?" / "I've been burned before."

> "That's a smart question — you should be careful. Here's what I can tell you: WCR Studios is a local company right here in South Jersey. We're not some agency in another state. You can Google us, check out our other sites, and I'll give you references from trades businesses we've already built for."

> "But you don't have to take my word for it — look at the site. It's already built. You can see exactly what you're getting before you spend a dime. And the guarantee is simple: you own everything. The website, the content, the domain — it's all yours. If you want to move it somewhere else in a year, you can. No lock-in."

---

## R — Reinforce the Decision

### Interested — Close and Onboard:

> "Great decision, {{First Name}}. Here's exactly what happens next: I'm going to send you a quick form to confirm your business details — hours, services, service area. Takes 3 minutes. Once I get that back, your site goes live within 48 hours. You don't have to do anything else."

> "I'll also set up your Google Business Profile if you don't have one — that's the listing that shows up in the map when people search. Between the site and the profile, you're going to start showing up for people who are actively looking for a {{Trade Type}} in {{City}}."

*(Get payment or send the invoice immediately. The sale isn't complete until they've taken the first action step.)*

### Warm but Not Ready:

> "No problem. I just texted you the preview link and I'll email the pricing breakdown. Take a look tonight, show your partner — and call or text me when you're ready. I'm going to follow up in a few days either way just so the site doesn't sit there. Sound good?"

*(Add to Day 2 follow-up sequence. Call on Day 5.)*

### Not Interested:

> "No worries at all, {{First Name}}. If you ever want a site or want to upgrade what you've got, my number's in your phone now. Appreciate your time."

*(Add to Day 7 follow-up sequence.)*

## Key Stats to Drop Naturally

Use when they fit the conversation — don't dump them all at once:

- "70% of local searches happen on mobile phones"
- "46% of all Google searches are looking for local businesses"
- "88% of people who do a local search on their phone call or visit within 24 hours"
- "The average cost per click for a Google Ad in trades is $6-$15 — a website gets you those clicks for free"

---

## After Every Call (Within 5 Minutes)

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Interested** | Send invoice + onboarding form. Schedule follow-up for 48 hours. |
| **Warm** | Text preview link + email pricing. Add to Day 2 follow-up. |
| **Cold** | Add to Day 7 follow-up sequence. |
| **No answer** | Leave voicemail: "Hey {{First Name}}, this is {{Rep Name}} from WCR Studios — I built a website for {{Business Name}} and wanted to get your take on it. I'll text you the link. Give me a call when you get a chance." Text the preview link immediately. |

**Speed to lead:** The gap between the call and the follow-up action should be under 5 minutes. Response rate drops 80% after 30 minutes.
