# Cold Call Script — South Jersey Trades Outreach

---

## Before You Call

- Have their preview site open on your screen so you can reference it
- Know their name, business name, trade, and city
- Call Tuesday–Thursday between 7:00–8:00 AM or 4:30–6:00 PM
- Stand up when you call — it changes your voice

---

## The Opening (First 10 Seconds)

> "Hey {{First Name}}, this is Wren. I'm a web developer here in South Jersey — I work with local trades guys like {{Trade Type}} businesses. I actually put together a website for {{Business Name}} and wanted to get your take on it. Got about two minutes?"

**If they say "Who is this?" / "What's this about?"**

> "Totally fair — I'm Wren, I build websites for trades businesses in South Jersey. I put together a site for {{Business Name}} to show you what I can do. I'm not selling anything right now — I just wanted your honest opinion on it."

**If they say "I'm busy"**

> "Completely understand. When's a better time to catch you for two minutes? I've got a site ready for you to look at — I think you'll like it."

---

## The Value Prop (30 Seconds)

> "So here's the deal — I noticed that when people in {{City}} search Google for '{{Trade Type}} near me,' you're not showing up the way you should be. I built you a professional site that's designed to fix that. It's mobile-friendly, loads fast, and it's set up to rank on Google so you get more calls from people who are already looking for exactly what you do."

> "I just texted/emailed you the link so you can check it out. Take a look when you get a chance — it's got your services, contact info, everything ready to go."

---

## Handling Objections

### "I don't need a website."

> "I hear that a lot, and I get it — you've built your business on word of mouth, and that's awesome. But here's the thing: even your referrals are Googling you before they call. If they search your name and nothing comes up, or they find a competitor with a nicer site, that's a lost job. This is just about making sure the customers who are already looking for you can actually find you."

### "I already have a website."

> "That's great — you're ahead of a lot of guys in your trade. Mind if I take a look? A lot of the sites I see for trades businesses were built a few years ago and aren't really set up for mobile or for Google anymore. If yours is solid, I'll tell you that. And if there are a few things that could bring in more calls, I can show you what that looks like. Either way, it's a free second opinion."

### "How much does it cost?"

> "Good question. I keep it simple — my starter package is $499 for the full website, design, and a year of hosting. No hidden fees, no monthly charges on that tier. I also have packages that include Google Business setup, ongoing SEO, and monthly updates if you want someone handling all of it. But the $499 option gets you a professional site that's live and working. Want me to send over the details?"

### "That sounds expensive."

> "I totally get it. Let me put it this way — if this site brings you even one extra job this year, it's paid for itself two or three times over. And most of my clients tell me they start getting calls within the first month. It's not an expense, it's an investment in getting your phone to ring more."

### "I'll think about it."

> "Absolutely, no rush at all. Tell you what — I'll send you the link to your preview site so you can look at it on your own time. If you like it, just call or text me back. The site is ready to go live whenever you are. Sound good?"

### "How do I know this is legit?"

> "Fair question. I'm a local guy — I live right here in South Jersey. I can send you links to other sites I've built for businesses in the area. You can also Google me or check out my portfolio. I'm not going anywhere — I work with local businesses because I want long-term relationships, not one-off sales."

---

## The Close

### If They're Interested:

> "Awesome. I can have your site live within 48 hours. Want to set up a quick 15-minute call so I can walk you through the site and any changes you'd want? What does your schedule look like tomorrow or Thursday?"

### If They're Warm but Not Ready:

> "No problem at all. I'll send you the preview link and my pricing info right now. Take a look, show it to your partner or whoever — and just give me a call or text when you're ready. There's no deadline on this. Sound good?"

### If They're Not Interested:

> "Hey, no worries at all. If you ever decide you want a site or want to upgrade what you've got, my number's right there. I appreciate your time, {{First Name}}. Have a good one."

---

## After the Call

- **Interested:** Send preview link + pricing one-pager within 5 minutes. Schedule follow-up.
- **Warm:** Send preview link + pricing. Add to Day 3 follow-up sequence.
- **Cold:** Add to Day 7 follow-up sequence. Move on.

---

## Key Stats to Drop Naturally

- "70% of local searches happen on mobile phones"
- "46% of all Google searches are looking for local businesses"
- "88% of people who do a local search on their phone call or visit within 24 hours"
- "A business without a website loses about 30% of potential customers"

---

## Tone Reminders

- Talk like a person, not a salesman
- Use their name — it builds trust
- Don't rush. If they want to talk about their business, let them
- Mirror their energy. If they're casual, be casual. If they're all business, match it
- Never trash their current site to their face — always frame it as "improvements"
- If they say no, be gracious. They might call back in 3 months
